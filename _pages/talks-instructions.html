Key Info For Speakers

Everything you need to know before presenting at Dot Net Liverpool ⚡

Welcome!

We’re really looking forward to seeing your talk at this month’s Dot Net Liverpool meetup event! This page explains how the evening will run, what we’ll need from you, and how to make sure everything goes smoothly on the night.

🎤 Arrival & Setup

Please arrive 15–20 minutes before the event starts. This gives us time to test your laptop, slides, or any demos before the talks begin.

If you have any special setup requirements (e.g., sound output, browser version, or device quirks), please let us know in advance so we can accommodate you.

💻 AV & Screen Sharing Setup

You can either use your own laptop, or send your slides to us beforehand and we can present them for you.

  • If using your own laptop: We’ll have an HDMI connection available (1080p). Please bring any adapters you might need (USB-C, DisplayPort, etc.).
  • If you’d like us to present your slides: Please send them at least 24 hours before the event so we can merge them into our master deck.

For screen sharing and live demos, we use VDO.Ninja. You can stream your screen by joining with the tag dotnetliverpool123, which connects directly to our livestream software.

VDO.Ninja Links:
  • View link: https://vdo.ninja/?view=dotnetliverpool123
  • Stream link: https://vdo.ninja/?push=dotnetliverpool123&q=0

📝 Before the Talk Starts

  1. Intro slide deck on repeat (every 15 seconds)
    Please open our intro slide deck in Google Slides: Intro Slides 2026.
    At the bottom-right of the slideshow controls, click the three dots (More options), set Change slide to every 15 seconds, then click Play so the deck loops automatically while people arrive.
  2. Have the Name Picker ready for the JetBrains licence draw
    Before you start your talk, open the Name Picker tool at /name-picker so it's ready to use at the end of your session to select the winner of the JetBrains licence.

🗣️ Talk Length & Format

As a standard, we recommend presentations run between 45 minutes and 1 hour 40 minutes. If your session is likely on the longer side, we suggest including a short break midway to keep everyone refreshed.

For shorter talks, we can often arrange another speaker to fill the second half of the evening — just let us know in advance so we can plan accordingly.

There’s no fixed format — slides, live demos, storytelling, or interactive coding sessions are all welcome. The main aim is to share something valuable, spark discussion, and inspire the community.

📽️ Recording & Livestream Consent

We often livestream and record our talks for the Dot Net Liverpool YouTube channel. By presenting, you’re giving us permission to include your talk in the livestream and future videos.

If you’d prefer not to be recorded or streamed, please email or message us before the event so we can make alternative arrangements (e.g., pausing the stream during your slot).

📑 Slide Presentations

If your talk only involves slides (no live demo), we may merge your slides into a shared presentation deck to avoid switching laptops between speakers — this keeps transitions smooth and the event flowing.

Please send slides in PowerPoint (.pptx) or PDF format to contact@dotnetliverpool.org.uk at least a day before the event if you'd like us to host them.

💬 Questions or Help

If you have any questions, you can post them in the #general channel on our Discord server. We’ll be keeping an eye there in the lead-up to the event and on the day itself.
